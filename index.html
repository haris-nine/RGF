Avoiding Default Judgement

If you fail to file the ET3 or file it late, the tribunal may rule against you without a hearing. This is called a "default judgement," where the claimant wins without you having the opportunity to defend yourself. A solicitor will ensure the ET3 is filed on time and correctly.

The ET3 forms the foundation of your defence. A solicitor's expertise will be crucial in crafting a well-reasoned and legally sound defence, setting the stage for a strong case.

Evidence and Witness Management

Negotiating Settlements

FAQs About Legal Representation in
Employment Tribunals

I've received an ET1 claim form. What is an ET3, and why is it important?
An ET3 is the response form you, as the employer, must file in response to an ET1 claim form from an employee or job applicant. The ET3 is crucial because it allows you to set out your defence against the claims made against you. This forms the foundation of your legal arguments moving forward.
How much time do I have to respond to an ET1 claim, and what happens if I miss the deadline?
You have 28 days from the date the tribunal sent you the ET1 claim form to file your ET3 response. Failure to file the ET3 on time or at all may result in a default judgment against you. This means the claimant will win their case without a hearing, and you will lose the opportunity to present your side of the story.
The ET3 seems complicated. Can I make changes to it later if I make a mistake?
Yes, you can apply to the tribunal to amend your ET3 if you need to correct errors or add information. However, the later you make the request, the less likely the tribunal will allow the amendment. It's best to get it right the first time with the help of a solicitor.
Will I have to pay the claimant's costs if they win the tribunal case?

How can a solicitor help me prepare the best possible defence for my ET3?
A solicitor specialising in employment law can provide expert advice on the best way to defend the claim. This includes analysing the ET1, identifying the key legal and factual issues, and helping you gather and present evidence effectively. They can also help you prepare your witnesses for the hearing and anticipate the claimant's arguments.
The claimant has indicated they're open to early conciliation. Do I still need a solicitor at this stage?
Yes, seeking legal advice even before engaging in early conciliation is recommended. A solicitor can advise you on the strengths and weaknesses of your case, potential risks, and the likely outcome of the claim. They can help you evaluate settlement offers and negotiate a favourable resolution, potentially avoiding the need for a tribunal altogether.
What happens after I submit the ET3?
After the tribunal receives your ET3, they will usually respond within 5 working days. They will either issue a case management order outlining a timetable for exchanging evidence or schedule a preliminary hearing. Preliminary hearings are used to clarify the issues in dispute and to help the tribunal manage the case effectively.
If the case goes to a final hearing, how can a solicitor help me during the tribunal process?
A solicitor will represent you at the final hearing, presenting your case, cross-examining the claimant's witnesses, and making legal arguments on your behalf. They will ensure you comply with tribunal procedures and effectively present your evidence to the judge.
